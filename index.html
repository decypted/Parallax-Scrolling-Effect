Anatomy of the Great Zimbabwe Empire

The Great Zimbabwe Empire was an African Iron Age city that existed in southeastern Zimbabwe, about 19 miles southeast of Masvingo. The empires governance system was run by a hereditary monarchy of Shona elite

Autopsy of the Empire

Great Zimbabwe was a medieval African city that was apart of a wealthy African trading empire that controlled much of the East African coast during the 11th to the 15th centuries C.E. The city was the capital of the Kingdom of Zimbabwe, which was a Shona (Bantu) trading empire. The elite of the Zimbabwe Empire controlled trade up and down the east African coast.

Head: Significant Leaders & Accomplishments

At the helm of Great Zimbabwe was a hereditary monarchy, where power was passed down within an elite family. This monarchy oversaw a parliament of influential families, ensuring a centralized yet somewhat inclusive governance structure.

Heart: Religious Beliefs

The people of the Great Zimbabwe Empire held a deep reverence for a supreme deity called Mwari. Their spiritual connection with Mwari was facilitated through intermediaries known as "mhondoros" and "svikiros," who acted as essentially bridges between the divine and the mortal realm. Beliefs that would encompassed matters like rainmaking, agricultural fertility, and even health.

Spine: Political Structure

At its core, the empire operated under a hereditary monarchy, where power remained within a single elite family. This monarch ruled with authority and tradition. Additionally, there was a council, we will referred to as the "parliament," comprised of influential people from families closely tied to the monarch. These people played advisory roles, contributing to the empire's governance. Aswell as that, In order manage the diverse regions within the empire, regional governors were appointed directly by the king. This i believe is to allowed for a balance between central authority and regional autonomy, to ensure unity and stability.
